**Title:** Show HN: AI4Change — Open-source initiative connecting global teams to solve real problems with AI

**Text:**

Hi HN,

I'm launching AI4Change — an open-source initiative where global teams use AI to solve real-world problems, inspired by Playing for Change (the project that connects musicians across the world to play one song together).

The core idea: real problems, named by real people, solved by distributed teams using AI as their shared instrument, shipped as open-source tools anyone can use.

**How the flywheel works:**

1. PROBLEM — Someone names a real, human problem (e.g., "smallholder farmers lose 30% of crops to disease they can't identify early enough")
2. SIGNAL — Community discusses, votes, and connects expertise
3. BUILD — A global team forms and builds with AI, asynchronously, in the open
4. SHIP — Ships as open source. Not a prototype — a tool people can use, fork, and improve
5. STORY — The build gets documented end-to-end, so the next person sees: "someone like me did this"
6. REPEAT — Each story inspires the next problem

**Why this, why now:**

AI is reaching capability thresholds faster than expected. A teacher in rural Indonesia can now analyze student data like a well-resourced school district. A health worker in Guatemala can pattern-match symptoms like a research hospital. But the people who benefit most from AI are still the least likely to shape how it's built.

The tools exist. The talent exists. What's missing is the connective tissue — a way to match problems with builders across borders, with open source as the default.
