# LinkedIn — How It Works

People keep asking: "How does AI4Change actually work?"

Here's the flywheel:

**1. PROBLEMS** — Someone names a real problem. Not a tech problem. A human one. "Smallholder farmers in East Africa lose 30% of crops to disease they can't identify early enough."

**2. COMMUNITY SIGNAL** — The community votes, discusses, refines. The best problems rise. Others connect their expertise: "I have agronomic data." "I've built image classifiers." "I can design a mobile-first interface."

**3. BUILD** — A global team forms around the problem. AI is the instrument. They build together — asynchronously, across time zones, in the open.

**4. SHIP** — Everything ships as open source. Not a prototype. Not a demo. A real tool that real people can use, fork, and improve.

**5. STORY** — We document the journey. Who built it. Why it matters. How it works. Storytelling isn't marketing — it's infrastructure. It shows the next person: "Someone like me did this."

**6. MORE PROBLEMS** — Each story inspires the next problem. The flywheel spins faster.

This is how a farmer in Kenya, a data scientist in Berlin, and a designer in Lagos end up building something no institution ever would.

Open source. Problem-first. Global by design.
