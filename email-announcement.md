**Body:**

Hi there,

I want to tell you about something I've been building — and invite you to be part of it from the very beginning.

It started with a Playing for Change video at 2am. If you haven't seen it: musicians from around the world — strangers who've never met — each play their instrument, and it all gets mixed into one song. A street musician in New Orleans. A guitarist in Barcelona. Drummers in Congo. Singers in South Africa. One song, built across the world.

It's one of the most beautiful things I've ever seen. And it made me think:

**What if we did this with problem-solving?**

That's AI4Change.

A farmer in Kenya who knows exactly why crops fail in laterite soil. A data scientist in Berlin who builds predictive models. A designer in Lagos who makes complex things simple. None of them can solve the problem alone. Together, with AI as their shared instrument, they build something no institution, no company, no government ever would.

And then they give it away. Open source. For everyone.

---

**Here's how it works:**

1. **Someone names a real problem** — not a tech problem, a human one
2. **The community rallies** — people vote, discuss, connect their expertise
3. **A global team builds** — with AI, across time zones, in the open
4. **It ships as open source** — a real tool anyone can use, fork, and improve
5. **The story gets told** — so the next person sees: "Someone like me did this"

Each story inspires the next problem. The flywheel spins faster.

---

**Why now?**

AI is crossing thresholds experts thought were a decade away. A teacher in rural Indonesia can now analyze student data the way a well-funded school district does. A community health worker in Guatemala can pattern-match symptoms like a research hospital. A small-scale farmer can access crop intelligence that used to require a corporate contract.

The tools exist. The talent exists globally. What's been missing is the bridge — a way to connect problems with builders, across borders, with open source as the default.

AI4Change is that bridge.
