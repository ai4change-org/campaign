## Tagline

## Description

**AI4Change** connects problem-solvers worldwide who use AI as their shared instrument to tackle real-world challenges — and ships everything as open source.

Inspired by Playing for Change (where musicians across continents play one song together), AI4Change does the same with problem-solving. A farmer in Kenya names a problem. A data scientist in Berlin starts modeling. A designer in Lagos builds the interface. Together, they create solutions no one could build alone.

**How it works:**

- **Problems first** — Real problems, named by real people. Not tech problems — human ones.
- **Community signal** — The community votes, discusses, and connects expertise.
- **Global build** — Distributed teams form and build with AI, across time zones, in the open.
- **Open-source ship** — Everything ships as open source. If it can't be forked, it isn't ours to give.
- **Storytelling as infrastructure** — Every build gets documented so the next person sees: "Someone like me did this."

AI is reaching capabilities that were thought to be a decade away. But the people who need it most are the least likely to shape how it's used. AI4Change is the bridge.

## Maker's First Comment

Hey Product Hunt! I'm Valter, the founder of AI4Change.

This started with a late-night Playing for Change video and a question: what if we connected problem-solvers the way PFC connects musicians?

After 13+ years building technology systems, I've seen the same pattern: the people who need AI the most are the least likely to have access, resources, or a seat at the table. The tools have caught up. The talent is global. What's missing is the bridge.

AI4Change is that bridge. We start with problems, not technology. We build in the open. And everything ships as open source — because the solutions belong to the communities that need them.

We're early. The first thing we need isn't code — it's problems. Real problems, from real people, that AI-powered teams can solve together.

What problem would you submit? I'd love to hear from this community.
